Ecological Dependencies

Moving from ornamental decoration to native, layered biodiversity.

Indian Roller

Coracias benghalensis

An indicator species of a healthy, multi-layered resort ecosystem. Impossible to attract with standard municipal horticulture.

1. The Canopy Layer (Shade & Shelter)

Tall native trees providing thermal regulation for guest pool decks while serving as perching and observation posts for indicator avian species.

2. The Understory (Acoustic Masking)

Mid-level shrubs and broadleaf flora that absorb mechanical resort noise while providing nesting safety and foraging grounds.

3. The Ground Layer (Soil Retention)

Replacing concrete drains with water-retaining natural groundcover. Filters rainfall runoff and creates micro-habitats for crucial insect life.
